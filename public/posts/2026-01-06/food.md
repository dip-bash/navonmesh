In the heart of Singapore, 30% of the city's greens are now grown in vertical farms. These indoor ecosystems use 95% less water than traditional agriculture and require zero pesticides. 

The "Farm-to-Table" distance has been reduced from hundreds of miles to just a few elevator floors. As urban populations continue to swell, the ability to grow calories where they are consumed will be the key to global food security.
