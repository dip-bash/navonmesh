As we establish the first pressurized greenhouses on the Red Planet, a new debate is blooming. Scientists are divided on whether we should introduce terrestrial DNA into Martian soil, or if we have a moral obligation to keep the planet "pristine." 

The ability to grow food in-situ is the cornerstone of Martian colonization, but it comes at a potential biological cost that we may not fully understand for centuries. This isn't just about survival; it's about our legacy as a multi-planetary species.
