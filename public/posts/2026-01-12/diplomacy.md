In the heart of Brussels, a new wave of diplomatic discourse is taking shape. Leaders from across the continent are meeting in hushed chambers to discuss the future of integrated trade and defense. 

This shift, while subtle, marks a significant departure from the isolationist rhetoric that dominated the previous decade. Experts suggest that the economic pressures of the digital age are forcing a pragmatic return to cooperation, proving that geography remains a powerful binder even in a hyper-connected world.

As we look toward the next decade, the ability to find common ground in a fragmented digital landscape will be the defining skill of the new diplomatic class.
